Acute Liver Failure

ICU Decision Engine — Diagnosis · Etiology · Management · Transplant Criteria

ALF — Definition (AASLD / INASL 2024)
Acute liver failure requires ALL of the following criteria
  • ①Coagulopathy — INR ≥1.5 (not correctable by vitamin K)
  • ②Hepatic Encephalopathy — any grade (I–IV) in the setting of acute liver injury
  • ③No pre-existing liver disease — except Wilson's disease, autoimmune hepatitis, or Budd-Chiari (which can present acutely on chronic disease)
  • ④Duration of illness ≤26 weeks from onset of jaundice to onset of encephalopathy
⚠️ALF ≠ ACLF: ACLF occurs in patients with pre-existing chronic liver disease / cirrhosis. Use CLIF-SOFA for ACLF, King's College / ALFED for ALF. Management differs significantly.

Etiology Workup — Key Differentiators
Wilson's Disease — Do Not Miss
  • 🔍Low ALP paradox: ALP normal or LOW in Wilson's ALF (ALP <40 IU/L in fulminant Wilson's) — classic differentiating clue
  • 🔍Coombs-negative haemolytic anaemia — look for elevated LDH, low haptoglobin, raised bilirubin out of proportion
  • 🔍Wilson's Index >11 (using bilirubin, AST, PT, ALP, WBC) = high mortality without transplant
  • 🔍Slit-lamp KF rings (absent in 50% of acute Wilson's — do not exclude diagnosis if absent)
Anti-TB DILI — Diagnosis & Management
  • 01STOP all anti-TB drugs immediately — INH, Rifampicin, Pyrazinamide, Ethambutol. Do not wait for workup.
  • 02Bilirubin >3× ULN + LFT rise >3× while on ATT = definite hepatotoxicity — stop all ATT
  • 03Continue TB management with streptomycin + ethambutol (hepatotoxicity-sparing regimen) if TB cannot be held
  • 04Rechallenge protocol: Wait until INR <1.5 and bilirubin falling. Reintroduce one drug at a time: Rifampicin first (week 1) → INH (week 2) → PZA last (often omitted permanently)
  • ⚠PZA is the most hepatotoxic — often permanently omitted. Extend regimen duration if PZA excluded.
HEV in Pregnancy
  • 🔴HEV in 3rd trimester pregnancy = extremely high mortality (20–25%). ICU admission mandatory for all.
  • 01No specific antiviral — supportive management. Delivery does NOT consistently improve maternal outcomes.
  • 02Multidisciplinary: Hepatology + Obstetrics + Neonatology. Fetal monitoring continuous.
  • 03DIC common — aggressive FFP, fibrinogen replacement. Low threshold for cryoprecipitate.
  • 04Delivery timing: obstetric indication only — no evidence forced delivery improves liver outcomes

ICP Prevention — All Grade III–IV
  • 01Head elevation 30° — maintain at all times. Avoid head-down positioning, tight ETT ties, PEEP >10 if avoidable.
  • 02Target Arterial Ammonia <150 µmol/L — CRRT is most effective at reducing ammonia rapidly. Lactulose via NG if not intubated. Rifaximin 550mg BD.
  • 03Hypertonic Saline (HTS) — target serum sodium 145–150 mmol/L. Give 20–30 mL boluses of 30% NaCl or continuous 3% NaCl infusion. Prophylactic hypernatraemia reduces cerebral oedema.
  • 04Strict normoglycaemia — glucose 6–10 mmol/L. Hypoglycaemia dramatically worsens cerebral oedema. Check hourly.
  • 05Hypothermia — consider mild hypothermia (35–36°C) as bridge to transplant in Grade IV with refractory ICP. Reduces cerebral metabolism and ammonia uptake.
  • 06Avoid hyponatraemia — maintain Na 145–150 mmol/L. Hyponatraemia <130 dramatically worsens cerebral oedema.
  • 07Seizure control — EEG monitoring in intubated patients (subclinical seizures common). Phenytoin or levetiracetam — avoid benzodiazepines if possible (increase encephalopathy).
  • 08Avoid nephrotoxins, hypotension — maintain MAP ≥75 mmHg (CPP = MAP − ICP, target CPP >50 mmHg). Noradrenaline if needed.
ICP Treatment — Acute Rises
  • 🔴Mannitol 20% — 0.5–1 g/kg IV bolus over 15 min. Repeat every 4–6h if serum osmolality <320 mOsm/kg. Hold if oliguria/anuria (accumulates).
  • 🔴HTS 30% — 30 mL bolus IV if mannitol contraindicated (AKI/oliguria) or in addition. Target Na 145–150 mmol/L.
  • 🔴Hyperventilation — temporary measure only. PaCO₂ 32–35 mmHg reduces ICP within minutes but effect wanes <30 min. Use only as bridge.
  • 🔴CRRT — most effective sustained ammonia reduction. High-volume CRRT (35–45 mL/kg/hr) with bicarbonate buffer.
  • 🔴Invasive ICP monitoring — consider epidural/intraparenchymal monitor if Grade IV + transplant candidate. Only where expertise available. High bleeding risk — correct coagulopathy first (FFP/PCC).
⚠️Do NOT use dexamethasone for cerebral oedema in ALF — no evidence of benefit, increases infection risk and may worsen outcomes.
N-Acetylcysteine (NAC) — All ALF
INASL / AASLD recommend IV NAC for ALL ALF regardless of etiology until INR shows improvement or liver transplantation. NAC improves transplant-free survival in non-paracetamol ALF (Lee 2009 NEJM).
NAC Protocol Reference
Paracetamol ALF — Standard 21h IV Protocol (Prescott)
  • Bag 1150 mg/kg in 200 mL 5% dextrose over 1 hour
  • Bag 250 mg/kg in 500 mL 5% dextrose over 4 hours
  • Bag 3100 mg/kg in 1000 mL 5% dextrose over 16 hours
✅For paracetamol ALF: If INR remains elevated after 21h, continue Bag 3 rate (6.25 mg/kg/hr) until INR <1.5 or transplant. Do not stop based on time alone.
Non-Paracetamol ALF — Prolonged Protocol (INASL 2024)
  • Day 1Loading: 150 mg/kg IV in 200mL 5% dextrose over 1h, then 50 mg/kg over 4h, then 100 mg/kg over 16h
  • Day 2+Maintenance: 100 mg/kg IV over 16–24h daily. Continue until INR improves (<2.0 on 2 consecutive days) or transplant
  • DurationContinue for minimum 5–7 days regardless of etiology. Do NOT stop because of clinical improvement alone — INR must confirm.
⚠️NAC anaphylactoid reactions: flushing, urticaria, bronchospasm (especially with loading dose). Slow infusion rate, give antihistamine if reaction. Do NOT stop permanently — restart at lower rate after reaction settles.
High-Volume Plasma Exchange (HVPE)
HVPE removes circulating toxins, inflammatory mediators, and replaces clotting factors. INASL 2024 + EASL 2017 recommend HVPE as bridge to transplant or recovery in ALF.
Indications for HVPE in ALF
  • ✅INR >2.0 despite NAC for ≥24h — progressive coagulopathy
  • ✅Grade III–IV encephalopathy not improving with standard management
  • ✅Progressive multiorgan dysfunction — as bridge to transplant
  • ✅Contraindication or unavailability of transplant — HVPE as definitive bridge
  • ✅Wilson's ALF — removes copper + inflammatory mediators. HVPE + chelation pending transplant.
  • 🚫Active uncontrolled sepsis — relative contraindication. Treat infection first, reassess.
  • 🚫Heparin allergy — use citrate anticoagulation instead
HVPE Volume Calculator
HVPE Protocol
  • 01Volume: 8–12 mL/kg/hr × 6–8 hours per session (total 1–1.5× plasma volume = ~40–60 mL/kg)
  • 02Replacement: FFP as primary. Can use albumin 5% for first 50% then FFP. Cryoprecipitate if fibrinogen <1g/L.
  • 03Sessions: 3 consecutive days (FULMAR trial). Reassess after each session — INR, encephalopathy, haemodynamics.
  • 04Anticoagulation: Citrate anticoagulation preferred in ALF (systemic heparin increases bleeding risk)
  • 05Monitor: Ionised calcium (citrate binds calcium — replace if Ca²⁺ <1.0 mmol/L), temperature, haemodynamics, INR post-session
📊Evidence: FULMAR trial (NEJM 2022) — HVPE 3×/day for 3 days in ALF improved 90-day transplant-free survival (58% vs 48%). INASL 2024 now recommends HVPE as standard of care in ALF with progressive organ dysfunction.
CRRT in ALF — Indications & Protocol
CRRT is preferred over intermittent haemodialysis in ALF — haemodynamic stability + sustained ammonia reduction + ICP control
Indications for CRRT in ALF
  • ✅Arterial ammonia >150 µmol/L — CRRT reduces ammonia rapidly (most effective intervention)
  • ✅AKI stage 2–3 — creatinine ×2 baseline or oliguria >12h despite resuscitation
  • ✅Refractory fluid overload — impacting oxygenation or abdominal compartment pressure
  • ✅Metabolic acidosis pH <7.2 refractory to medical management
  • ✅Electrolyte emergencies — K⁺ >6.5, hyponatraemia <125 mmol/L
  • ✅Grade III–IV HE + rising ammonia — even without AKI, CRRT for ammonia control is justified

Etiology-Specific Management
Acute Hepatitis B ALF
  • 01Start Tenofovir disoproxil 300mg OD immediately — reduces viral load, may improve outcome. Continue indefinitely post-transplant.
  • 02Entecavir 0.5mg OD (1mg if decompensated) — alternative if Tenofovir not available
  • ⚠Do NOT use interferon in HBV-ALF — risk of flare and death
  • 03Screen family members — HBsAg, anti-HBs. Vaccinate unprotected contacts.
Autoimmune Hepatitis (AIH) ALF
  • 01Exclude active infection before steroids — blood cultures, CXR, urine culture, CMV/EBV PCR
  • 02If no infection: Methylprednisolone 40–60mg IV OD × 5–7 days
  • 03Response criteria: MELD/INR improving at 5–7 days → continue steroids. No response → stop, list for transplant.
  • ⚠Grade IV encephalopathy + AIH — steroids unlikely to help. Prioritise transplant listing over steroid trial.
Wilson's ALF
  • 🔴High mortality without transplant — list immediately on diagnosis. Wilson's Index >11 = transplant essential.
  • 01D-Penicillamine NOT recommended in acute Wilson's — worsens haemolysis, poorly tolerated. Use only for maintenance.
  • 02Plasma exchange (HVPE) — removes circulating copper + inflammatory mediators. 3–5 sessions as bridge to transplant.
  • 03Trientine (oral chelation) — can be used alongside HVPE if patient can swallow
  • 04Haemolysis management: Transfuse packed cells to maintain Hb >8 g/dL. Avoid haemodynamic instability.
Budd-Chiari Syndrome (Acute)
  • 01Anticoagulate immediately — LMWH (enoxaparin 1 mg/kg BD) even with coagulopathy. Thrombosis extension worsens outcomes more than bleeding risk.
  • 02Thrombolysis (catheter-directed) — if within 2 weeks of onset and accessible clot
  • 03TIPS — refractory to anticoagulation or liver failure progression. Reduces portal pressure immediately.
  • 04Thrombophilia screen: JAK2 V617F, Factor V Leiden, antiphospholipid antibodies, PNH screen, protein C/S/antithrombin
Paracetamol ALF
  • 01NAC is specific antidote — start immediately regardless of time since ingestion. Better prognosis than non-paracetamol ALF.
  • 02Plot paracetamol level on Rumack-Matthew nomogram (see NAC tab for dosing)
  • 03Good prognosis with NAC + supportive care. Transplant-free survival 60–70% with NAC.
  • 04Psychiatric assessment mandatory post-recovery (intentional overdose assessment)
Supportive Management — All ALF
Nutrition in ALF
  • 01Do NOT restrict protein — protein restriction worsens encephalopathy (muscle catabolism → more ammonia). Target 1.2–1.5 g/kg/day protein.
  • 02Energy: 25–35 kcal/kg/day. Avoid overfeeding.
  • 03Route: Oral or NG (Grade I–II HE). NG in Grade III (NGT placement usually safe). TPN only if EN absolutely contraindicated.
  • 04Branched-chain amino acids (BCAA) — consider if protein intolerant. Not routinely needed.

⚠️Do NOT routinely correct INR with FFP — INR is the most important prognostic marker in ALF. Correcting it removes the ability to monitor disease progression and falsely reassures. Only give FFP for active bleeding or invasive procedure.

ALFED Score — Indian-Specific
ALF Early Dynamic score — developed and validated in Indian cohorts (SGPGI/AIIMS). More sensitive than KCC in Indian patients (HEV dominant). INASL 2024 recommends ALFED alongside KCC.
🇮🇳ALFED advantage in India: KCC was developed in UK (paracetamol-dominant). In India where HEV/ATT DILI dominate, ALFED has higher sensitivity (85% vs 65% for KCC) for predicting poor outcome. Use BOTH KCC + ALFED when listing for transplant.
